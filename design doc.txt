IRON VALE - WORKING DESIGN DOC
Last updated: Apr 2026


CORE GAME
Iron Vale is a progressive action RPG built in HTML5 Canvas.
The game has 11 stages.

Modes:
  Normal:
    Death sends the player to game over and progress is lost.

  Softcore:
    Death restarts the current stage.
    The player keeps level, stats, skills, talents, and pendants.
    Current-level EXP progress is reset to 0.

  Dev Test:
    Lets the player choose class, level, stat allocation, skill points, talents,
    primary attribute after Plasticity, pendants, and starting stage.

Between stages:
  Rest: full heal, current-level EXP progress becomes 0.
  Continue: keep current HP and EXP.
  The between-stage screen also shows stats, skill tooltips, talent information,
  and pendant inventory.


STATS
Strength:
  +10 max HP per point.
  +5 current HP when allocated.
  Scales damage if it is the hero's primary stat.

Agility:
  +5% movement speed per point.
  Scales damage if it is the hero's primary stat.

Intelligence:
  -0.2s cooldown per point, minimum 0.5s.
  Scales damage if it is the hero's primary stat.

Primary stat:
  Barbarian starts with STR.
  Rogue starts with AGI.
  Mage starts with INT.
  Plasticity allows the player to choose any primary stat.


HEROES
Barbarian:
  STR 10 / AGI 8 / INT 4, HP 100, primary STR.
  Q Slash: 1x STR frontal attack.
  W Whirlwind: 1.5x STR circular AoE.
  E Slam: 1.75x STR circular AoE, slows enemies, brief immunity.
  R Berserk: 10s buff, swaps to berserk sprites, takes 1.5x damage.
    Lv1: 1.75x damage dealt.
    Lv2: 2x damage dealt.
    Does not change model size, range, or hitboxes.

Rogue:
  STR 6 / AGI 10 / INT 6, HP 60, primary AGI.
  Q Slash: 1x AGI frontal attack.
  W Throw: 1x AGI fast dagger projectile in facing direction.
  E Wind Walk: 10s double speed and enemies lose tracking.
    Entry animation plays on cast.
    Damaging ability breaks it and plays exit animation.
    Slash gets bonus damage when it breaks Wind Walk:
      Lv1 +1.75x primary stat, Lv2 +2.05x, Lv3 +2.35x.
  R Slice & Dice: 3x AGI AoE around hero.
    Plays Rogue Slash sound 3 times with 0.15s spacing.

Mage:
  STR 4 / AGI 6 / INT 12, HP 40, primary INT.
  Q Blastwave: 1x INT frontal attack.
  W Fireball: 1.75x INT projectile with explosion AoE.
    Projectile sound stops on impact.
  E Frost Nova: 1.75x INT AoE, 20% larger radius.
    Applies freeze: 1s stun, then 1s slow.
    Frozen/slowed monsters are tinted blue.
  R Blink: teleports up to 8x hero dimensions in facing direction.
    Lv2 grants a second charge after 1s; cooldown begins after second charge.


LEVELING
EXP to level is 100 flat.
Monster EXP decays over time: base * 0.5^(aliveFrames / 2700), minimum 1.

On level-up:
  1. Choose a skill upgrade.
  2. Spend 3 stat points.
  3. At levels 3, 7, and 10, choose a talent.

Skill caps:
  Q/W/E max level 3.
  R max level 2.
  Skill upgrades multiply current damage by 1.3.

Input safety:
  Pendant drops, level-up screens, and talent screens pause gameplay.
  Talent selection requires confirmation so arrow-key stat allocation does not
  accidentally level the first talent.


TALENTS
Offensive:
  Lv3 Haste: all cooldowns reduced by 1s.
  Lv7 Bloodlust: 10% lifesteal on attacks and skills.
  Lv10 Avatar: doubles hero model size and all attack/skill ranges.

Defensive:
  Lv3 Stoneskin: 15% reduced incoming damage.
  Lv7 Lifeblood: regenerates 1% max HP every 10s in stage.
  Lv10 Gift of the Ancients: doubles max HP.

Utility:
  Lv3 Plasticity: choose primary stat and gain +3 bonus stat points per level.
  Lv7 Balance: +10 all stats.
  Lv10 Nature's Swiftness: doubles movement speed.


PENDANTS
Mini-boss pendants pause the game and show a full notification.
Pendant icons appear on the HUD and can be hovered for tooltips.

Stage 3 Minotaur drop table:
  Pendant of Vitality: +50 max HP.
  Pendant of Swiftness: +20% move speed.
  Pendant of Acuity: all cooldowns -1s.

Stage 8 Mimic drop table:
  Pendant of Might: +10 STR and +25% skill/range area.
  Pendant of the Assassin: +10 AGI and next damaging skill hit adds +15 damage every 15s.
  Pendant of Enlightenment: +10 INT and absorbs one hit every 10s.


STAGES
Stage 1 - Goblin Swarm:
  6 goblins, 10 HP, 10 damage.
  Pack Courage: goblins near 2+ goblins move faster and attack sooner.
  Coward Scatter: nearby goblins briefly flee when one dies.
  Uses Stage 1 art and play-area blockers from the blue reference.
  Door is not physically blocked; it only opens when clear.

Stage 2 - Clay Golems:
  3 golems, 150 HP.
  Chase player, slam AoE, slow hero, enrage at 40% HP.
  Cracked earth/rubble hazards can remain after slams.
  Uses Stage 2 art and play-area blockers from the blue reference.

Stage 3 - Minotaur:
  1 minotaur, 500 HP.
  Charge has roar on windup, direction lock, and clear telegraph.
  Crashing into walls or stage blockers stuns the minotaur.
  Uses Stage 3 art and top door trigger with no bottom/door blockers.

Stage 4 - Skeleton Crypt:
  6 skeletal archers plus 2 skeletal champions.
  Archers flee, strafe, and shoot.
  Champions are slow/tanky guards that stand apart to protect archers.
  Champions can block attacks once every 5 seconds.
  Uses Stage 4 art with no blockers.

Stage 5 - Orc Grunts:
  4 normal orcs plus 1 brute orc.
  Brute is double size with double HP.
  Orcs have overhead cleave, low-HP charge, and shield block variant.
  Shield block plays Shield block.wav.
  Cleave hitbox range is shortened from the first implementation.
  Uses Stage 5 art with no blockers.

Stage 6 - Trolls:
  2 trolls, 200 HP.
  Trolls immediately aggro from stage start.
  Throw boulders at the hero's ground position.
  Rock shadow/marker shows where it will land.
  Landing plays Ground Smash.wav, deals AoE damage, and leaves slowing rubble.
  Boulder sprite:
    Row 1 position 1 is the flying projectile frame.
    Remaining 15 cells are the fast impact animation.
  Uses Stage 6 art and Troll art transition image.

Stage 7 - Ghoul Pit:
  4 waves of increasing ghoul counts.
  Next wave spawns only after all current ghouls are killed.
  Ghouls spawn from side pits with warning shadow and Ghoul pit intro sound.
  Ghouls stay just outside the hero sprite so Q attacks can target them.
  Feeding Frenzy: ghouls that hit the player gain brief speed.
  Ghoul EXP is reduced to one-third of the earlier value.
  Uses Stage 7 art and Ghoul Pit Art transition image.

Stage 8 - Mimic:
  Mimic immediately runs toward the hero when the stage starts.
  HP is increased to 2x.
  Movement speed scales from the hero's speed, but is tuned down from the fastest version.
  Mimic uses all available class skills with half the hero's cooldowns.
  Mimic uses class sprite animations where available.
  Barbarian mimic can cast Berserk and swap to berserk sprites.
  Mimic tries to close distance before attacking to reduce easy kiting.

Stage 9 - Twin Guardians:
  2 guardians, double size, 1000 HP each.
  Fast spear attack with clear forward projectile/extension.
  Spear projectile sheet is 3600x1600, 6 frames, one row, 600px per frame.
  Guardians try to attack from offset positions.
  If one dies, the survivor enrages with speed/range/damage pressure.

Stage 10 - Iron Tribunal:
  Iron Sentinel, Chain Warden, Ash Priest, and supporting tribunal behavior.
  Iron Sentinel is slow but deals high damage; attack uses slam sound.
  Chain Warden is faster and uses chain/slow mechanics.
  Slow effect should avoid the old blue body outline.
  Ash Priest HP: 250.
  Ash Priest heal: 100.
  Uses Stage 10 art and Iron Tribunal transition art.

Stage 11 - John Pork:
  Final boss stage.
  Entering stage 11 pauses normal music.
  Shows Game Art/John Pork.png.
  Loops sounds/Voice lines/facebook_call.mp3.
  After 10 seconds, the stage starts and the ring sound stops.
  Normal music remains paused during the John Pork stage.

SPRITE AND COLLISION NOTES
Standard monster sheets:
  2048x2048, 4 columns x 4 rows, 512x512 per frame.
  Row 1/down, row 2/up, row 3/right, row 4/left.

Master monster sheets:
  4096x4096, four 2048x2048 quadrants.
  Top-left run, top-right attack, bottom-left block, bottom-right death.

Collision:
  Hero and monster blocker collision uses a small center-bottom footprint,
  not the full visible sprite rectangle.
  Hero collision slides along blockers rather than hard-stopping on the first blocked axis.
  Monster AI also uses blocker-aware movement so enemies do not blindly walk through walls.
